Goals

  • Appreciate
  • Understand
  • Identify
  • Engage
  • Assess
Love philosophy is too much to expect. Appreciate, then. Understand how philosophy is a necessary part of life. Identify the major figures in philosophy and their key ideas. Learn how to engage in philosophical dialogue. Assess philosophical assumptions and arguments.

Questions

  • What can we know?
  • What is real?
  • What are we?
  • What should we do?
  • What is the meaning of life?
These are the main questions that we will focus on this semester.

Success

What Success Is Not

Getting a grade.
A C does not mean that you did not succeed. An A does not mean that you did succeed.

What Success Is

Being faithful stewards of the gifts that God has given you, as you faithfully do the task to which God has called you.
God has given each of you certain gifts and talents. You probably know what some of them are. You'll identify others during your time at OBU. Some of you have a talent for philosophical thought. My job is to help you cultivate it. Others of you may not have that talent. My job is to help you get better at it — because you will have a better life if you can think well.

Secrets for Success!

Not my rule. You can choose to follow it or not. If you choose not to follow the rule, here's what will happen. The dean of the business school will report it to my dean. I will then get in trouble with my dean, which will make me angry, then I go to my office and make up your exam.
To succeed you need to be here. You won't be able to just read the text and pass the exams. Being here, though, means more than just showing up. Be present, be ready — don't be like this guy. When I say something stupid, you're welcome to laugh at me. The fact that you laughed shows that you listened. Two things I don't like: sleeping in class and having conversations while I'm talking. It's disrespectful, don't do it.
I don't ban electronic devices. We may use them for polls and for some learning checks. Research is showing that taking notes on computers is not ideal for learning. Don't be doing something on your device while in this class that has nothing to do with the class. Don't think you're fooling anybody.
You've heard it said that the only dumb question is an unasked quetion. That's not exactly true. Do ask quetions. If you don't understand something, then odds are most of the class doesn't either. The more you engage the ideas the better you will do.
I will help you — come to office hours, those lonely, lonely office hours. Just stop in and say hello.
The student handbook defines academic misconduct. For us, academic integrity means doing your own work. Don't cheat on exams, don't get papers from the internet. If you use a source, then cite it. (An exception is the apology paper.)
We'll have fun this semester. Maybe not this much fun, but we will have fun. Engage the material, enjoy it, and the grade tends to work out fine.
